Mapping Marine Diversity

Explore the hidden patterns of life beneath the waves. This visualization uses data from the Ocean Biodiversity Information System (OBIS) to model ecological communities across different ocean depths.
Click anywhere on the ocean to start.

How to use:

  • Explore the global distribution of marine life.
  • Click anywhere on the map to select a location.
  • Scroll down to "dive" into the water column and see how diversity changes with depth.

Methodology & Model

This visualization is powered by a predictive model of marine ecological communities derived from the Ocean Biodiversity Information System (OBIS).

Data Processing

Raw species occurrence data was filtered into four depth zones: Epipelagic (0-200m), Mesopelagic (200-1000m), Bathypelagic (1000-4000m), and Abyssopelagic (4000m+). For each zone, we identified the top 100 most abundant species to characterize the community structure.

Clustering & Prediction

We applied K-Means clustering to Hellinger-transformed abundance matrices to identify distinct ecological communities. To create a continuous global map, we used K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) spatial interpolation based on 3D coordinates derived from latitude and longitude.
